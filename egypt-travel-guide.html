Things To See

01
Giza Plateau, Cairo

The Giza Pyramid Complex & Sphinx

The last surviving wonder of the ancient world needs no introduction ‚Äî but standing before Khufu's pyramid in person still defies expectation. The Sphinx crouches nearby, carved from a single limestone ridge over 4,500 years ago, its gaze fixed eternally eastward. Allow a full day to absorb the scale and silence of this sacred plateau.

02
Aswan, Upper Egypt

Abu Simbel Temples

Ramesses II's colossal rock-cut temples ‚Äî relocated wholesale in a UNESCO engineering miracle to save them from Lake Nasser's rising waters ‚Äî rank among Egypt's most breathtaking sights. Four 20-metre statues of the pharaoh guard the entrance. Twice a year, sunlight penetrates the inner sanctuary to illuminate the gods within. Extraordinary.

03
Giza, Cairo

The Grand Egyptian Museum

The largest archaeological museum on Earth, opened in 2023, houses over 100,000 artefacts including the complete treasures of Tutankhamun ‚Äî his golden death mask, gilded throne, and more. Plan at least five hours; many visitors return for a second day. The building itself, with its translucent stone facade and view of the pyramids, is a masterpiece.

04
West Bank, Luxor

Temple of Hatshepsut

Carved dramatically into the limestone cliffs of Deir el-Bahari, this three-tiered mortuary temple was built for ancient Egypt's most powerful female pharaoh. The colonnaded terraces, vivid carved reliefs, and sheer audacity of its cliff-face setting make it one of the ancient world's great architectural achievements ‚Äî and one of Egypt's most photogenic.

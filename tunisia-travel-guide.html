Religion & Culture

Tunisia is a Muslim-majority country but one of the most secular in North Africa. Dress modestly when visiting religious sites. During Ramadan, restaurants close by day. Alcohol is available in hotels and licensed restaurants. Bargaining is expected in souks.

Getting Around

Taxis are cheap and plentiful in cities â€” always ask for the meter. The TGM train connects Tunis to Sidi Bou Said, Carthage, and La Marsa. Renting a car is recommended for the south and interior. Drones are prohibited throughout Tunisia.

Safety

Tunisia is generally safe for tourists. Petty theft (phone snatching) is the main concern. Tourist police operate in most major sites. Avoid remote border areas near Libya and Algeria. Solo female travelers should be extra aware in crowded medinas.

Language & Connectivity

Arabic and Tunisian dialect are official, but French is widely spoken. English is increasingly understood in tourist areas. SIM cards (Orange, Tunisie Telecom) are available at the airport and are very affordable. Purchase one immediately on arrival.

Tunis-Carthage (TUN) is the main international airport with direct flights from most European cities. Monastir and Djerba also receive seasonal European charter flights. Ferries sail from Marseille (22h) and Palermo (11h). Consider flying into one airport and out of another.

No mandatory vaccines required from most Western countries, though hepatitis A is recommended. Tap water is safe in cities but many prefer bottled. Travel insurance is strongly recommended. Pharmacies are well stocked and easy to find.
